<p><b>Gammalsvenskby</b> (<a href="/wiki/Gammalsvenska" title="Gammalsvenska">Gammalsvenska</a>: <i lang="sv">Gammölsvänskbi</i>, <small><a href="/wiki/Literal_translation" title="Literal translation">lit.</a>&#8201;</small>&#39;Old Swedish Village&#39;; <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Старошведське</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Ukrainian" title="Romanization of Ukrainian">romanized</a>:&#160;</small><span title="Ukrainian-language romanization"><i lang="uk-Latn">Staroshvedske</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Alt-Schwedendorf</i>) is a former village that is now a neighbourhood of <a href="/wiki/Zmiivka,_Kherson_Oblast" class="mw-redirect" title="Zmiivka, Kherson Oblast">Zmiivka</a> (Ukrainian: <span lang="uk">Зміївка</span>) in <a href="/wiki/Beryslav_Raion" title="Beryslav Raion">Beryslav Raion</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kherson_Oblast" title="Kherson Oblast">Kherson Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>. It was briefly known as <b>Verbivka</b> (Ukrainian: <span lang="uk">Вербівка</span>) prior to being integrated with Zmiivka. Gammalsvenskby is known for its <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Swedes" title="Estonian Swedes">Estonian Swedish</a> <a href="/wiki/Kinship_and_descent" class="mw-redirect" title="Kinship and descent">cultural heritage</a>.
</p><p>Zmiyivka also includes three former villages settled by ethnic <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">Germans</a>: The Lutheran villages of Schlangendorf and Mühlhausendorf and the Roman Catholic village of Klosterdorf. In the nineteenth century, the whole region, and large parts of southern Russia, contained villages settled by Germans belonging to various Protestant faiths, particularly <a href="/wiki/Lutherans" class="mw-redirect" title="Lutherans">Lutherans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mennonites" title="Mennonites">Mennonites</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholics</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Askania-Nova" title="Askania-Nova">Askania-Nova</a> <a href="/wiki/Biosphere_reserve" class="mw-redirect" title="Biosphere reserve">biosphere reserve</a> is located near the village.
</p><p>In April 2022, Russian military forces reached Gammalsvenskby as part of the <a href="/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine">Russian invasion of Ukraine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SD20220415_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SD20220415-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The village remained occupied until 11 November 2022, when it was reclaimed by the Ukrainian army.<sup id="cite_ref-TT20221111_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TT20221111-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After the Ukrainian liberation, Russia has repeatedly <a href="/wiki/Attacks_on_civilians_in_the_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" title="Attacks on civilians in the Russian invasion of Ukraine">targeted the civilian population</a> in Gammalsvenskby with bombings including use of <a href="/wiki/White_phosphorus_munitions" title="White phosphorus munitions">white phosphorus munitions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resettlement_of_Estonian_Swedes_and_founding_of_Gammalsvenskby">Resettlement of Estonian Swedes and founding of Gammalsvenskby</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gammalsvenskby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Resettlement of Estonian Swedes and founding of Gammalsvenskby">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Capitulation_of_Estonia_and_Livonia" title="Capitulation of Estonia and Livonia">Capitulation of Estonia and Livonia</a></div>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Oest.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Oest.jpg/200px-Oest.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="154" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Oest.jpg/300px-Oest.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Oest.jpg/400px-Oest.jpg 2x" data-file-width="654" data-file-height="502" /></a><figcaption>Dagö, Estonia</figcaption></figure>
<p>The population of Gammalsvenskby traces its origins to <a href="/wiki/Hiiumaa" title="Hiiumaa">Hiiumaa</a> (Dagö) in present-day <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, once a part of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Estonia_(1561%E2%80%931721)" title="Duchy of Estonia (1561–1721)">Duchy of Estonia</a>. Under the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Nystad" title="Treaty of Nystad">Treaty of Nystad</a>, the island was among the territory ceded to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> in 1721 at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_War" title="Great Northern War">Great Northern War</a>.
</p><p>A few decades later, a portion of the peasant population in conflict with the local aristocracy, answered <a href="/wiki/Catherine_the_Great" title="Catherine the Great">Catherine the Great</a>'s 1762 <i><a href="/wiki/Ukase" title="Ukase">ukase</a></i> calling for settlers in <a href="/wiki/Novorossiya" title="Novorossiya">Novorossiya</a> on territory newly conquered from the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>; today this land is in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ukraine" title="Southern Ukraine">Southern Ukraine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-argsweua_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-argsweua-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Enticed by promises of new fertile land along the <a href="/wiki/Dnieper" title="Dnieper">Dnieper</a>, about 1,200 people departed Dagö on 20 August 1780, and trekked overland to Novorossiya, arriving on 1 May 1781.<sup id="cite_ref-Geisinger1975_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geisinger1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rudling2005_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rudling2005-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Only about 400 Swedes remained behind in Dagö.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> While some sources call the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Swedes" title="Estonian Swedes">Estonian Swedes</a>' migration an outright expulsion from their Estonian homeland, other accounts stress the fact that these poor and oppressed <a href="/wiki/Serf" class="mw-redirect" title="Serf">serfs</a> were given what may have seemed like a generous offer.
</p><p>Regardless of the impetus, the outcome of this mass migration was disastrous. Of the 1,200 villagers who left Estonia, only 900 made it to Novorossiya.<sup id="cite_ref-argsweua_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-argsweua-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Geisinger1975_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geisinger1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On arrival, there was no trace of the houses they had expected to find. Moreover, during their first year in Ukraine, an even larger portion of the settlers died, mainly due to <a href="/wiki/Dysentery" title="Dysentery">dysentery</a>. That first year, 318 died along with another 116 the following year. By 1794, only 224 people remained in Gammalsvenskby. In 1802, the Russian government ordered all male Swedes to marry by the age of 30 in an effort to boost the population.<sup id="cite_ref-Geisinger1975_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geisinger1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Maintaining_the_Swedish_heritage">Maintaining the Swedish heritage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gammalsvenskby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Maintaining the Swedish heritage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1787 to 1805, <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea_Germans" title="Black Sea Germans">German colonists</a> were invited to Gammalsvenskby to bolster the region's population. The Germans referred to the area as the "<span title="German-language text"><span lang="de" style="font-style: normal;">Schwedengebiet</span></span>" (<i>Swedish District</i>) and the village as "Alt-Schwedendorf". They soon founded three neighbouring villages: Schlangendorf, Mühlhausendorf, and Klosterdorf. With the arrival of these Germans, the Swedes were quickly outnumbered and eventually many of the area's pastors and teachers were German-speakers who did not know Swedish.<sup id="cite_ref-Geisinger1975_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geisinger1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the Swedes did not make full use of the <a href="/wiki/Arable_land" title="Arable land">arable land</a> they had been allocated — they focused their industry more on fishing than farming<sup id="cite_ref-Geisinger1975_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geisinger1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> — the reallocation of farm land to the German newcomers strained relations between Gammalsvenskby's Swedes and their German neighbors, although intermarriage between the communities did occur, as is evidenced by parish register entries for weddings in both communities' churches.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> While the Swedes and Germans were sometimes rivals, they were never enemies and the two communities cooperated when times were bad.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite this, the people of Gammalsvenskby maintained their traditions, <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Sweden" title="Church of Sweden">Church of Sweden</a> <a href="/wiki/Lutheranism" title="Lutheranism">Lutheran</a> faith, and <a href="/wiki/Gammalsvenska" title="Gammalsvenska">old Swedish dialect</a>. At the end of the 19th century, some ties with Sweden were re-established, with the Ukrainian Swedes viewed as a "lost tribe" that preserved older Swedish traditions, such as writing in <a href="/wiki/Runes" title="Runes">runes</a> and maintaining an older form of the Church of Sweden's liturgy.<sup id="cite_ref-Forge_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forge-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Prince_Carl,_Duke_of_V%C3%A4sterg%C3%B6tland" title="Prince Carl, Duke of Västergötland">Prince Carl</a> raised more than 6,000 rubles in Sweden and <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> to support construction of a new Swedish church in the village to replace the previous wooden church given by <a href="/wiki/Prince_Potemkin" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Potemkin">Prince Potemkin</a> that burned in the mid-19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The new parish church of St. John opened in 1885. For a time, before the revolutions that followed World War I, visits from Sweden became frequent, and some villagers subscribed to <a href="/wiki/Swedish_newspapers" class="mw-redirect" title="Swedish newspapers">Swedish newspapers</a>.
</p><p>Despite this, there were efforts by Russia to better integrate the Ukrainian Swedes with the Russian Empire. The original settlement plans exempted Ukrainian Swedes from conscription into the tsar's army, but this changed by the end of the 1800s and 130 men from Gammalsvenskby were inducted into the Russian army during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AtWar_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtWar-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relocation_attempt_to_Sweden">Relocation attempt to Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gammalsvenskby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Relocation attempt to Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Svenskybykarikatyr.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Svenskybykarikatyr.png/220px-Svenskybykarikatyr.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="187" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Svenskybykarikatyr.png/330px-Svenskybykarikatyr.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Svenskybykarikatyr.png/440px-Svenskybykarikatyr.png 2x" data-file-width="643" data-file-height="547" /></a><figcaption>Caricature of the Gammalsvenskby returnees published in the Swedish Communist newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Folkets_Dagblad_Politiken" title="Folkets Dagblad Politiken">Folkets Dagblad Politiken</a></i>, August 1929. The picture portrays the settlers as entertainers, being put to display at a community fair in <a href="/wiki/Ljungby" title="Ljungby">Ljungby</a>.</figcaption></figure>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">Russian Civil War</a>, Gammalsvenskby was largely held by the Red Army, although the village did come under artillery fire from the White Army under General <a href="/wiki/Anton_Denikin" title="Anton Denikin">Anton Denikin</a>. After fighting moved away from the villages in 1921, villagers sought aid from Sweden, including writing to <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Uppsala" title="Archbishop of Uppsala">Archbishop of Uppsala</a> <a href="/wiki/Nathan_S%C3%B6derblom" title="Nathan Söderblom">Nathan Söderblom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman2000_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman2000-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 1922, the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Red_Cross" title="Swedish Red Cross">Swedish Red Cross</a> led an expedition to Gammalsvenskby to provide aid and guidance in developing the region and its farmland. Under this plan, two new Swedish villages, Nysvenskby ("New Swedish Village") and Svenskåker ("Swedish Field"), were established in part to preserve their right to the land. The neighboring German villages similarly established additional outposts, Friedenheim and Neuklosterdorf.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman2000_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman2000-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Conflicts with Soviet authorities over taxation, <a href="/wiki/Collectivization_in_the_Soviet_Union" title="Collectivization in the Soviet Union">collectivization policies</a>, and the right to maintain their Lutheran faith increased the efforts by some villagers to seek return to Sweden. On 1 September 1927, 136 farmers from the village petitioned "the people of Sweden, Finland, and America" for aid to reunite them with their fellow Swedes. These efforts were not immediately embraced by Sweden's representative to Moscow, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Gerhard_von_Heidenstam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carl Gerhard von Heidenstam (page does not exist)">Carl Gerhard von Heidenstam</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://sv.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_von_Heidenstam" class="extiw" title="sv:Carl von Heidenstam">sv</a>&#93;</span>, who urged caution.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman2000_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman2000-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 28 June 1928, 429 villagers voted to emigrate back to Sweden under the <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_the_Rights_of_the_Peoples_of_Russia" title="Declaration of the Rights of the Peoples of Russia">Declaration of the Rights of the Peoples of Russia</a>'s support for ethnic self-determination. At this time, pressure in Sweden to allow the return of the people from Gammalsvenskby increased, and on 22 February 1929, the <a href="/wiki/Riksdag" title="Riksdag">Riksdag</a> approved their right to come to Sweden. By June 1929, the Soviet government reached an agreement with the Swedes regarding disposition of their property in Ukraine and passport fees, and most of the people of Gammalsvenskby began preparing to leave.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman2000_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman2000-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The villagers could only take with them what could be packed on a passenger train.<sup id="cite_ref-Geisinger1975_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geisinger1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 22 July 1929, the Swedes of Gammalsvenskby who had received an exit permit were brought downriver to <a href="/wiki/Kherson" title="Kherson">Kherson</a> on two steamers. From there, the Swedish Red Cross brought them on the cargo ship <i>Firuzan</i> to <a href="/wiki/Constan%C8%9Ba" title="Constanța">Constanța</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Romania" title="Kingdom of Romania">Romania</a>, where the overland journey began. They travelled by train through Hungary and Austria to Germany, passing through <a href="/wiki/Sinaia" title="Sinaia">Sinaia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bra%C8%99ov" title="Brașov">Brașov</a>,  <a href="/wiki/L%C5%91k%C3%B6sh%C3%A1za" title="Lőkösháza">Lőkösháza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Passau" title="Passau">Passau</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stralsund" title="Stralsund">Stralsund</a> on the way to <a href="/wiki/Sassnitz" title="Sassnitz">Sassnitz</a>. From there, they took the ferry across the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> to Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On 1 August 1929, 885 Ukrainian Swedes arrived in <a href="/wiki/Trelleborg" title="Trelleborg">Trelleborg</a>, Sweden, where they were received by <a href="/wiki/Prince_Carl,_Duke_of_V%C3%A4sterg%C3%B6tland" title="Prince Carl, Duke of Västergötland">Prince Carl, Duke of Västergötland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-argsweua_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-argsweua-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Of those who opted to remain in Gammalsvenskby, 19 families (94 people) soon moved on to <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, where earlier emigrants from Gammalsvenskby had settled. Six of these families later returned to Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The majority of the villagers stayed in Sweden, many of them settling in <a href="/wiki/Gotland" title="Gotland">Gotland</a>, as well as in <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterg%C3%B6tland" title="Västergötland">Västergötland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sm%C3%A5land" title="Småland">Småland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In an effort to integrate these "ancient Swedes" with modern Sweden, officials did not allow them to stay in a single, common settlement.<sup id="cite_ref-RedSweden_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RedSweden-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Instead, the government took a very paternalistic approach towards the Gammalsvenskby emigrants, requiring them to apprentice with established farmers to learn Swedish agricultural practices.<sup id="cite_ref-Hedman_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hedman-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>About four months after arriving in Sweden, some emigrants requested to return to Ukraine. Peter Knutas and Waldemar Utas wrote to the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic">Ukrainian SSR</a> that the move to Sweden was a thoughtless step and sought permission for three families to return to Ukraine.<sup id="cite_ref-Forge_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forge-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Some emigrants also joined the <a href="/wiki/Left_Party_(Sweden)" title="Left Party (Sweden)">Communist Party of Sweden</a> in hope of reflecting their loyalty to Soviet authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-argsweua_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-argsweua-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The movement of Ukrainian Swedes — both to Sweden and then back to the Ukrainian SSR — was used for propaganda purposes by both anti-Soviet and pro-Soviet media.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Soviet_repression.2C_Holodomor.2C_and_World_War_II"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_repression,_Holodomor,_and_World_War_II">Soviet repression, Holodomor, and World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gammalsvenskby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Soviet repression, Holodomor, and World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Holodomor" title="Holodomor">Holodomor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Great_Purge" title="Great Purge">Great Purge</a></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Minnesm%C3%A4rke.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Minnesm%C3%A4rke.jpg/220px-Minnesm%C3%A4rke.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Minnesm%C3%A4rke.jpg/330px-Minnesm%C3%A4rke.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Minnesm%C3%A4rke.jpg/440px-Minnesm%C3%A4rke.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1600" /></a><figcaption>Memorial to the 16 men and one woman from Gammalsvenskby who were killed or disappeared in the Stalinist purge of 1937–1938.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></figcaption></figure>
<p>In total, around 250 villagers chose to return to Gammalsvenskby. With the support of the Communist Party of Sweden, they established a minor <a href="/wiki/Collective_farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Collective farm">collective farm</a> called <i>Röd Svenskby</i> (Red Swedish Village).<sup id="cite_ref-RedSweden_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RedSweden-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Life in the Soviet Union turned out to be hard. In 1929, the church in Gammalsvenskby was closed by the Soviet government. The <a href="/wiki/Holodomor" title="Holodomor">famine of 1932–1933</a> renewed interest in the idea of returning to Sweden, and some villagers signed a list stating that they wanted to leave the country. This led to the arrest of 20 people by the secret police, the <a href="/wiki/State_Political_Directorate" title="State Political Directorate">GPU</a>. Five of them were sent to prison. Several villagers were killed in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Purge" title="Great Purge">Stalinist purge</a> of the following years. In the 1930s, the majority of the 3,500 Scandinavian descendants living in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ukraine" title="Southern Ukraine">Southern Ukraine</a> were accused of spying and sent with their families to <i><a href="/wiki/Katorga" title="Katorga">katorga</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karelia" title="Karelia">Karelia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-argsweua_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-argsweua-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With the <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Nazi invasion of the Soviet Union</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">German army</a> arrived in Gammalsvenskby on 25 August 1941, where the soldiers were welcomed as liberators.<sup id="cite_ref-AtWar_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtWar-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> During the Nazis's three-year occupation, they granted the Swedish Ukrainians German citizenship and many of the men from Gammalsvenskby joined the German forces — both voluntarily and through conscription. As Soviet troops advanced in October 1943, Swedes and Germans were removed from the <a href="/wiki/Reichskommissariat_Ukraine" title="Reichskommissariat Ukraine">Reichskommissariat Ukraine</a> under <a href="/wiki/German_evacuation_from_Central_and_Eastern_Europe" title="German evacuation from Central and Eastern Europe">Germany's evacuation plans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AtWar_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtWar-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Many evacuees from Gammalsvenskby ended up in <a href="/wiki/Krotoszyn" title="Krotoszyn">Krotoschyn</a>, in the German <a href="/wiki/Reichsgau_Wartheland" title="Reichsgau Wartheland">Warthegau</a> that had been annexed from <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. Nearly 150 residents of Gammalsvenskby were caught by Soviet authorities at the end of the war and sent to <a href="/wiki/Labor_camps" class="mw-redirect" title="Labor camps">labor camps</a>, but were allowed to return to Ukraine as early as 1947. Others managed to go to Sweden or directly back to Gammalsvenskby.<sup id="cite_ref-RedSweden_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RedSweden-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1947, under a Soviet policy to remove Germanic place names, Schlangendorf became <i>Zmiivka</i>, Mühlhausendorf became <i>Mykhailivka</i>, and Klosterdorf became <i>Kostirka</i>. Gammalsvenskby was renamed <i>Verbivka</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-RedSweden_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RedSweden-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1951, after the <a href="/wiki/1951_Polish%E2%80%93Soviet_territorial_exchange" title="1951 Polish–Soviet territorial exchange">exchange of territories by Poland and the Soviet Union</a>, around 2,500 people were relocated to the area from the <a href="/wiki/Drohobych_Oblast" title="Drohobych Oblast">Drohobych Oblast</a> villages of Lodyna, Dolyshni Berehy, and Naniv. Due to the resulting increase in population, the four villages were united under the name Zmiivka. With this migration, Zmiivka became home to the largest <a href="/wiki/Boykos" title="Boykos">Boykos</a> (Ukrainian Highlander) diaspora in Kherson Oblast, making up nearly 80% of the villagers. The newly relocated populace was officially prohibited from celebrating their traditional holidays, such as <a href="/wiki/Vertep" title="Vertep">Vertep</a> during <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>. To make matters worse, the locals among whom they were settled considered the newcomers to be anti-Soviet nationalists.<sup id="cite_ref-Cybriwsky2018_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cybriwsky2018-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1955 a dam was constructed on the Dnieper, creating the <a href="/wiki/Kakhovka_Reservoir" title="Kakhovka Reservoir">Kakhovka Reservoir</a>. This submerged part of the village along with several islands and fishing waters.<sup id="cite_ref-Forsman2016_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forsman2016-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Prior to the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Soviet_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Fall of the Soviet Union">fall of the Soviet Union</a>, contacts with Sweden and Canada were re-established, and in the 1990s the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Sweden" title="Church of Sweden">Church of Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gotland_Municipality" class="mw-redirect" title="Gotland Municipality">Gotland Municipality</a>, and other Swedish organizations lent economic support and led relief efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chumak_(company)" title="Chumak (company)">Chumak</a>, a Swedish-owned producer of oil, ketchup and canned food, was established in the nearby town of <a href="/wiki/Kakhovka" title="Kakhovka">Kakhovka</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In early October 2008, King <a href="/wiki/Carl_XVI_Gustaf" title="Carl XVI Gustaf">Carl XVI Gustaf</a> and Queen <a href="/wiki/Queen_Silvia_of_Sweden" title="Queen Silvia of Sweden">Silvia</a> visited Zmiivka and Gammalsvenskby as part of a <a href="/wiki/State_visit" title="State visit">state visit</a> to Ukraine.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gammalsvenskby&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the village had only around 108 people who share a Swedish cultural heritage. Only a few of them still speak the local Swedish dialect fluently and German is often used instead. However, the Swedish heritage is reflected in Zmiivka's emblem, which incorporates the Swedish national symbol (the <a href="/wiki/Three_Crowns" title="Three Crowns">Tre Kronor</a>), as well as a blue cross on a yellow field, which inverses the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Sweden" title="Flag of Sweden">Swedish flag</a>'s colors. On 15 April 2001, Gotland signed a <a href="/wiki/Sister_city" title="Sister city">sister city</a> agreement with the village.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Tourism from Sweden remains an important aspect of the village's economy and an impetus for preservation of the <a href="/wiki/Gammalsvenska" title="Gammalsvenska">Gammalsvenska dialect</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The whole of <a href="/wiki/Beryslav_Raion" title="Beryslav Raion">Beryslav Raion</a> is now heavily <a href="/wiki/Ukrainization" title="Ukrainization">Ukrainianized</a> due to the resettlement of many people from western Ukraine in the region, including to the local villages of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lvivski_Otruby&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lvivski Otruby (page does not exist)">Lvivski Otruby</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9B%D1%8C%D0%B2%D1%96%D0%B2%D1%81%D1%8C%D0%BA%D1%96_%D0%9E%D1%82%D1%80%D1%83%D0%B1%D0%B8" class="extiw" title="uk:Львівські Отруби">uk</a>&#93;</span>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lvove&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lvove (page does not exist)">Lvove</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9B%D1%8C%D0%B2%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B5_(%D0%91%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B8%D1%81%D0%BB%D0%B0%D0%B2%D1%81%D1%8C%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B9_%D1%80%D0%B0%D0%B9%D0%BE%D0%BD)" class="extiw" title="uk:Львове (Бериславський район)">uk</a>&#93;</span>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tarasa_Shevchenka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tarasa Shevchenka (page does not exist)">Tarasa Shevchenka</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%A2%D0%B0%D1%80%D0%B0%D1%81%D0%B0_%D0%A8%D0%B5%D0%B2%D1%87%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%BA%D0%B0_(%D0%91%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B8%D1%81%D0%BB%D0%B0%D0%B2%D1%81%D1%8C%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B9_%D1%80%D0%B0%D0%B9%D0%BE%D0%BD)" class="extiw" title="uk:Тараса Шевченка (Бериславський район)">uk</a>&#93;</span>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine">2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gammalsvenskby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine">2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine</a>, Gammalsvenskby was occupied by Russian troops,<sup id="cite_ref-SD20220415_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SD20220415-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> who later destroyed a monument dedicated to Ukrainian independence in the village.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/2022_annexation_referendums_in_Russian-occupied_Ukraine" title="2022 annexation referendums in Russian-occupied Ukraine">2022 annexation referendums in Russian-occupied Ukraine</a>, the Russian occupiers shut off the internet and mobile communications in the village, and the population of Gammalsvenskby refused to take part in the balloting. People reportedly locked themselves in their homes, refusing to open their doors, although some people did manage to vote no.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2022_Ukrainian_southern_counteroffensive" class="mw-redirect" title="2022 Ukrainian southern counteroffensive">2022 Ukrainian southern counteroffensive</a>, the village was about 10 to 15 kilometres (6.2 to 9.3&#160;mi) southeast of the frontlines, and it was reported that the population was hoping for liberation by Ukrainian forces.<sup id="cite_ref-Radio_2022_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Radio_2022-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The village was liberated by the Ukrainian army on 11 November 2022, following Russian troops' withdrawal to the left (east) bank of the Dnipro.<sup id="cite_ref-TT20221111_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TT20221111-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After the liberation by Ukrainian forces, Russia has continued to target the village, in one attack destroying the local school in a bombing attack that killed one and wounded one of the school's employees. The village has also been the target of several attacks by Russia making use of <a href="/wiki/White_phosphorus_munitions" title="White phosphorus munitions">white phosphorus munitions</a> to deliberately cause as much <a href="/wiki/Attacks_on_civilians_in_the_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" title="Attacks on civilians in the Russian invasion of Ukraine">devastation to the local civilian population</a> as possible. These attacks have been described as direct <a href="/wiki/Terror_Bombing" class="mw-redirect" title="Terror Bombing">terror bombings</a> targeted at civilians as the village isn't of any particular military value. These bombings are under investigation for  possible <a href="/wiki/War_crimes_in_the_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" title="War crimes in the Russian invasion of Ukraine">war crimes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Russian drones have also targeted individual civilians walking the streets, as well as humanitarian aid cars. Due to the constant bombings, and lack of electricity and water, many residents have fled to neighbouring villages.<sup id="cite_ref-Nyheter_2023_w908_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nyheter_2023_w908-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to Gammalsvenskby's close proximity to the <a href="/wiki/Kakhovka_Dam" title="Kakhovka Dam">Kakhovka Dam</a>, its <a href="/wiki/Destruction_of_the_Kakhovka_Dam" title="Destruction of the Kakhovka Dam">destruction</a> created another water supply threat for the village.<sup id="cite_ref-Nerikes_Allehanda_2023_w763_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nerikes_Allehanda_2023_w763-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
